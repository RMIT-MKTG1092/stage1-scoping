Stage 1 Scoping Worksheet

Week 2 version. Use this worksheet to move from a broad opportunity direction to a clearer and more defensible opportunity space.

How to use: complete the sections in order. This is a scoping tool, not a full business case. In Week 2, you are judging whether an opportunity looks worth taking forward.

What you are doing this week

  • clarifying the opportunity space
  • bringing across the strongest evidence from Week 1
  • checking category, competitors, alternatives, and market gap
  • using light rough-cut numbers to test plausibility
  • making a simple gate decision

Important: you are not writing a finished product concept or a full financial model. Keep the work strong, clear, and evidence-based.

3. Rough-Cut Numbers (Week 2 version)

Use rough-cut numbers to test scale and plausibility. Keep them directional. If exact data is not public, use a clearly labelled estimate and explain your logic.

Week 2 rule: you only need:

  • TAM
  • SAM
  • SOM
  • one public benchmark
  • one feasibility note
MetricFigureSource / logic
TAM – Total Addressable Market
Broad market size if we look at the full category.
SAM – Serviceable Available Market
The part of the market that is actually relevant to your segment, category, or geography.
% of TAM
SOM – Serviceable Obtainable Market
A small and realistic share that could be reached first.
% of SAM
One public benchmark
Example: price point, average spend, growth rate, adoption rate, or market trend.
One feasibility note
One practical issue that could affect delivery.
