Rough-Cut Numbers

Use sources such as Euromonitor Passport or IBISWorld and cite them below.

Metric (definition & cue)FigureSource / Justification
TAM – Total Addressable Market
“Size of the entire market if we owned 100 % share.”
Units: annual $ revenue OR total units per year.
Where to look: industry reports, gov stats.
Formula: customers × spend / year.
SAM – Serviceable Addressable Market
“Portion the current model can actually serve.”
Units: $ or units per year inside scope.
Need: TAM × geography / segment filter.		 %
SOM – Serviceable Obtainable Market
“Realistic share we can win in 3 yrs.”
Units: $ or units per year we expect to win.
Need: credible market-share assumption.
Formula: SAM × expected share (%).		 %
CAC – Customer Acquisition Cost
“Cost to acquire one paying customer.”
Units: $ per acquired customer.
Need: marketing spend ÷ new customers.
CLV – Customer Lifetime Value (gross margin)
“Gross profit from an average customer over their lifetime.”
Units: $ gross margin per customer.
Need: avg revenue, variable cost, lifetime.
Formula: (Rev – cost) × lifetime – CAC.
